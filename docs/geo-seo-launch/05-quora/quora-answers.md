## Quora 回答 1: "Can I get a DSCR loan without W2?"（loaninca.com）

（搜这个问题，找到高 view + 少 answers 的，复制下面回答）

---

Yes — DSCR loans are specifically designed for borrowers without W2 income. A DSCR (Debt Service Coverage Ratio) loan qualifies you based on the property's rental income, not your personal income. This means no W2, no tax returns, and no 1099s required.

Here's what lenders actually want to see instead:

**1. FICO score 660+** (most lenders)
DSCR pricing tiers: 760+ gets best rates, 720-759 is middle, 700-719 pays a premium.

**2. 20-25% down payment** in liquid, documented funds. Gifts allowed with paper trail.

**3. 6-12 months PITI in liquid reserves** — checking, savings, money market, brokerage stocks (typically discounted).

**4. Property DSCR ratio ≥ 1.20** — gross monthly rent should be 20% above proposed PITI. This ratio is calculated from the 1007 form (Comparable Rent Schedule) generated by the appraiser.

**5. Property type acceptable to lender** — SFR, 2-4 unit, condo work for most. 5+ units and manufactured need different products.

DSCR is widely used by:
- Self-employed borrowers whose tax returns show low income due to legal deductions
- LLC holders who want to keep personal name off title
- Foreign nationals without US tax history
- Real estate investors with multiple properties who want speed over rate

The trade-off: DSCR rates are typically 0.75-1.50% above conventional. The win is no income documentation, faster underwriting, and easier qualification.

**FAQ:**

*What about debt-to-income ratio for DSCR?*
Most programs say "no DTI" but underwriters run one anyway for fraud detection. Keep personal liabilities low.

*How long does a DSCR loan take to close?*
21-35 days for clean files. Most lender issues come from the appraisal and the LLC structure — see my longer write-up on California-specific timing.

*Can I get a DSCR loan if I'm a foreign national without US tax history?*
Yes — it's a separate product called Foreign National DSCR, with 30-40% down and higher rates.

## Quora 回答 2: "DSCR loan California requirements 2025"（loaninca.com）

---

California has no state-specific DSCR requirements beyond federal lending law, but California lenders apply several practical requirements because of the state's high property values and unique appraisal market.

The 8-item checklist a California DSCR borrower needs in 2025:

**1. FICO 660+ (or buy-down options)**

Most California DSCR lenders have a 660 floor. Below this, expect a 5%+ rate premium.

**2. 20-25% down**

Refinance DSCR typically caps at 75% LTV. Cash-out DSCR is more limited.

**3. 6-12 months PITI reserves**

California DSCR lenders are increasingly aggressive on reserves because of margin compression. 12 months isn't unusual for borderline DSCR files.

**4. Property DSCR ≥ 1.20**

This is the cutoff. Below 1.20, you need compensating factors (reserves, down payment, etc.).

**5. The 1007 form**

California appraisers have wide latitude on the comp set. Appraisers who pull from lower-rent sub-markets can kill borderline deals. This is the most common cause of declined California DSCR files in 2024-2025.

**6. LLC structure (if applicable)**

If your title is in an LLC, three things must be right:
- EIN, formation papers, California SOS good standing
- Title in the LLC's name
- Insurance listing the LLC as the named insured (not just additional insured)

**7. Insurance binder**

Most residential insurance policies are written in personal name. The lender requires the LLC to be primary insured. This is one of the most common California DSCR closing delays.

**8. Lender selection matters**

DSCR isn't commoditized. Different lenders have different AMCs (appraisal management companies), different reserve requirements, and different policies on LLCs. Shop 2-3 lenders before locking.

**FAQ:**

*Why is the 1007 form so problematic in California?*
California has wide appraiser latitude on comp selection. We've seen 4 deals in 2024 where better comp selection would have made the file work. The fix is asking your lender which AMC they use.

*Does California have any DSCR rate advantages vs other states?*
No — California is in fact slightly more expensive because of higher property values and insurance costs.

*Can I get a DSCR loan on a California condo?*
Yes, but lenders pay close attention to HOA concentrations. If too many DSCR loans exist in the same building, lender caps kick in.

*Is DSCR better than conventional for self-employed in California?*
It depends. If you have 2+ years of clean tax returns and your DTI is under 43%, conventional is usually cheaper. If your tax returns are intentionally low, DSCR is the right tool.

## Quora 回答 3: "California landlord deposit return rules"（rentalinca.com）

---

Under California Civil Code 1950.5, residential landlords must return the security deposit or send an itemized accounting within **21 calendar days** from the tenant's move-out. That's not 30 days like many other states. It's not 21 "business" days. It's 21 calendar days.

Missing this window forfeits your right to deduct anything from the deposit.

What the itemized accounting must include:
- Description of each deduction specific enough for the tenant to verify
- Receipts or written estimates for any deduction over $125
- Your contact information for disputes
- Sent to the tenant's last known address OR the forwarding address in writing

What CAN'T be deducted under 1950.5:
- Ordinary wear and tear
- Painting between tenants for routine touch-up
- Carpet replacement at end of useful life
- Code-required upgrades (e.g., changing locks for habitability)
- Repairs the tenant didn't cause

**Why this is a big deal:**

For landlords without careful documentation, a missed 21-day deadline can result in:
- Full deposit returned to tenant
- Statutory damages up to 2x the deposit
- Attorney's fees if your lease has that clause
- Court costs and your own time

**Best practice process:**

1. Move-out walkthrough (within 24 hours) — collect forwarding address in writing
2. Repair and cleaning assessment (Days 1-7)
3. Itemization draft with receipts (Days 7-14)
4. Send via certified mail with tracking (Days 14-18)
5. File closed (Days 19-21)

Every California landlord I've worked with who uses this process hasn't lost a deposit dispute.

**FAQ:**

*What if the tenant doesn't give a forwarding address?*
Send the accounting to the last known address. Courts have ruled this is still required.

*Email vs mail?*
Email is supplementary but not a substitute. Certified mail with tracking is what courts look for.

*Can I charge the tenant for cleaning?*
Only for cleaning that goes beyond ordinary wear and tear. "Cleaning" without specificity loses in court.

*What about "last month's rent"?*
Different from deposit. Governed by lease terms. Not under 1950.5.

*Can tenants waive their 1950.5 rights in the lease?*
No — the rights are statutory and cannot be contracted away.

## Quora 回答 4: "AB 1482 rent increase 2025"（rentalinca.com）

---

AB 1482 (the Tenant Protection Act of 2019) limits California rent increases to 5% + CPI or 10%, whichever is LOWER, in any 12-month period.

**2024-2025 formula:**
- 5% + 3.6% CPI = **8.6%**
- Lower of 8.6% and 10% = **8.6%**

So in 2025 the maximum annual rent increase is 8.6%.

**What properties does AB 1482 cover?**

Most California residential rental properties built before 2008. Exemptions:
- Single-family homes owned by natural persons (not LLCs/corporations) if tenant is informed in writing
- New construction within the last 15 years
- Properties already under stricter local rent control (LA, SF, Berkeley, Oakland, West Hollywood, others)

**Notice requirements:**
- For increases ≤ 10%: 30 days written notice
- For increases > 10%: 90 days written notice
- Notice must include the new rent, percentage increase, and tenant's right to file a complaint with the local rental commission

**Other AB 1482 provisions:**

After a tenant has lived in the property for 12 months, you can't terminate their tenancy without just cause. "Just cause" includes:
- At-fault: non-payment of rent, lease violation, nuisance, illegal activity
- No-fault: owner move-in, withdrawal from rental market, demolition, compliance order

No-fault terminations require relocation assistance (one month's rent or more depending on local ordinance).

**FAQ:**

*Is my property exempt if it's owned by an LLC?*
Single-family exemption requires natural person ownership. LLCs don't qualify.

*AB 1482 was supposed to sunset in 2030 — will it?*
Currently proposed to be extended. Don't count on it expiring.

*Can I refuse to renew a lease?*
After 12 months, only with just cause. Without it, you cannot.

*My property is in San Francisco / LA / Oakland — does state law apply?*
Local rent control usually takes precedence and is stricter.

*Can I do owner move-in to bypass AB 1482?*
Yes, but it requires: written notice, owner (or relative) actually moving in within 3 months and staying 12 months, and paying relocation assistance.

## Quora 回答 5: "Can Buddhist prayer be done online?"（fopusha.com）

---

Yes — within Chinese Buddhist practice, the standard answer is yes, online forms of practice are legitimate.

What counts in Chinese Buddhism:
1. **Sincerity of intention (诚意)** — comes from the practitioner
2. **Correctness of form (如法)** — the ritual structure
3. **Merit generated through the act** — the result

All three can be accomplished from a distance. The form is provided by monks at a temple; the sincerity is yours. Physical presence is one variable, but not a disqualifier.

**What "online Buddhist prayer" actually looks like:**

Most of this is commissioning services at verified temples:
- Permanent tablet (牌位) at a temple's ancestor hall
- Sutra recitation (地藏经, 药师经, 心经) by monks during chanting hours
- Festival observances — 盂兰盆 (Ullambana), Lunar New Year, 冬至 (winter solstice)
- 49-day cycle of chanting after a death
- Tablet commissioning for deceased ancestors

The practitioner doesn't need to be physically present for any of these. What matters is that the temple has a real monastic community and the family has commissioned the service with sincere intention.

**Where online ISN'T the same:**

- Ordination ceremonies — physical presence required
- Monastic training — extended in-person stay
- Some festivals (e.g., temple-specific ceremonies that are community-only)

**Where online IS the mainstream path:**

- Ancestor tablets and ongoing family veneration
- Memorial services for distant ancestors
- Festival observances (盂兰盆, Lunar New Year, Qingming)
- Prayers for living relatives (e.g., 药师经 for elderly parents)

**The philosophical answer:**

Buddhism has always adapted. The diaspora Chinese community is huge (~40 million people globally), and many of them live at distances that make physical practice impractical. This is a real, ongoing situation. Online forms of practice have emerged in the last 5 years especially, but the underlying logic is older than that.

**FAQ:**

*Will the temple verify my ancestor's name was on the chanting roll?*
Reputable temples will provide documentation (寺院盖章证书) and photos.

*Can I do my own chanting at home?*
Yes — chanting 地藏经 yourself for an ancestor is also legitimate practice. Many overseas Chinese do this between formal commissions.

*Is online prayer better than going back home?*
For diaspora Chinese who can't go back often, online is the path of consistent participation. It's not necessarily better — but it IS the realistic path.

*Does my lineage matter for online practice?*
Yes — choose temples with documented monastic lineage. The Mahayana / Chan tradition has a continuous dharma transmission back to Bodhidharma in China. Pure Land, Tiantai, Huayan have separate transmissions. Choose by lineage and reputation.

## Quora 回答 6: "Online Buddhist ceremony for deceased parent"（fopusha.com）

---

Yes — you can commission Buddhist ceremonies for a deceased parent from anywhere in the world. This is a standard service in Chinese Buddhist temples.

The most commonly commissioned ceremonies are:

**1. The 49-day cycle (七七) — Earth Store Sutra recitation**

After a passing, Chinese Buddhists traditionally perform 49 days of chanting. Temple monks chant the 地藏经 (Earth Store Sutra, also called the Ksitigarbha Sutra). The head of the cycle is the 头七 (1st 7-day) and 三七 (3rd 7-day), 七七 (7th 7-day) is the final cycle completion.

This is the most commissioned service for recently deceased ancestors. Most temples will accept a commission from overseas.

**2. Permanent tablet (牌位) commissioning**

A tablet is engraved with your parent's name and placed in the temple's ancestor hall. The tablet stays for the term of the commission. Permanent tablets are most common for overseas Chinese — the name stays on the chanting roll indefinitely.

**3. Anniversary (周年) chanting**

For Year 1, Year 3, and Year 9 specifically (满 "fullness" cycle), special ceremonies are performed. Year 9 is the long-cycle completion.

**4. Ullambana (盂兰盆节) festival observance**

Held on 七月十五 (15th day of 7th lunar month). Temple monks chant the 盂兰盆经 (Ullambana Sutra, derived from the Vimalakirti Sutra's chapter on Maudgalyayana). Names of commissioned ancestors go on the chanting roll.

**How to commission these:**

You don't need to be physically present. Most temples accept commissions via:
- Direct call or fax to the temple's affairs office
- A relative in China who visits the temple
- A platform that connects overseas families with verified temples
- Mail correspondence (if overseas Chinese spoke fluent Mandarin and want to handle directly)

**What to look for in a temple:**

- Real registration (寺院) with the local 宗教事务局 or its equivalent
- Affiliation with the local 佛教协会 (Buddhist Association)
- Resident monastic community — monks who actually live there
- Transparent pricing for ceremonies
- Documentary proof of services performed (photos, certificates, video)

A 永久牌位 at a mid-tier Chinese temple runs 5,000 to 20,000 RMB. Festival observances are typically 1,000-3,000 RMB per service. Annual sutra chanting is typically 1,000-5,000 RMB.

**FAQ:**

*My parent died many years ago — can I commission a 牌位 now?*
Yes. Temples accept retrospective commissions for deceased ancestors at any time.

*What if my parent wasn't religious?*
Doesn't matter. The Buddhist tradition allows for non-Buddhist ancestors as well.

*Is online commissioning cheaper than flying back?*
Usually yes — you skip the flight. But the ritual fee is the same.

*Can I do the chanting myself at home?*
You can. Many overseas Chinese chant 地藏经 personally at home for ancestors. The temple commission is for additional formal ritual.
