I just reviewed our 2,000th California DSCR loan file.

In 12 years of doing this work, I want to share the **3 things brokers don't tell you** about closing DSCR loans in California — especially if you're an LLC holder, self-employed, or foreign national.

### 正文

Here's the reality: most "DSCR approved" loans never close. Why? Three reasons brokers usually don't surface upfront:

**1. The 1007 form is 80% of the deal.**

The URLA-1007 (Single Family Comparable Rent Schedule) is generated by the appraiser — not you, not us, not the broker.

What I see kill deals:
- Comps pulled from a lower-rent submarket 2-3 miles away
- Concessions (2 weeks free rent) that aren't called out
- Condition adjustments flagged but not adjusted in the rent roll

When we push back with our own rent analysis (Zillow Rent Index + 90-day MLS rentals in the same ZIP), about 60% of the time the appraisal gets revised.

**2. Your LLC structure matters MORE than the broker said.**

If you're holding property in an LLC, three things have to be right at closing:
- Title is in the LLC's name (not your personal name)
- Underwriter has formation papers, EIN, CA SOS good standing
- Insurance has LLC as the named insured — NOT an additional insured

Most small investors skip item #3. It kills deals 3 days before close.

**3. DSCR is a tool, not a destination.**

The investors who build real wealth with DSCR in California do this:
- Acquire with DSCR (no tax returns needed)
- Wait for 1-2 years of LLC income on file
- Refinance into conventional when rates align
- Use cash-out refi to acquire the next property

This is the ladder. Pure DSCR held to maturity is expensive — typically +1.00-1.50% rate vs conventional.

## LinkedIn 帖 2：rentalinca.com — California 小房东

---

### 标题（hook 前 3 行）

Small California landlords are losing $5,000-$30,000 per violation — and most don't know it.

I'm a small California landlord (4 units, since 2018). I've processed 100+ tenant move-outs. Here's the **6-item legal checklist that actually protects you** under California law:

### 正文

If you own 1-10 California rental units and don't have a property manager or lawyer on retainer, this is the floor of what you need.

**1. Security deposit accounting on a 21-day clock.**

Under California Civil Code 1950.5(e), you have 21 calendar days from tenant move-out to either:
- Return full deposit, OR
- Send an itemized accounting + receipts

Miss this by even 24 hours? You forfeit the right to deduct anything.
Penalty: full deposit back + potential treble damages + attorney's fees.

**2. AB 1482 rent increase cap.**

Covers most properties built before 2008 (not exempt).
2024-2025 max annual increase: 5% + CPI (3.6%) = **8.6%**.

Notice: 30 days for ≤10% increase, 90 days for >10%.
If your property is in LA / SF / Berkeley / Oakland — local rent control usually takes precedence and is stricter.

**3. Habitability documentation.**

California Civil Code 1941 sets the bar. Civil Code 1942.4 added mold specifics.
Your evidence:
- Move-in condition report (signed)
- Dated photos
- All repair requests with timestamps
- All completion records

If you can't prove you met habitability, you can't evict for non-payment.

**4. Entry notice.**

Civil Code 1954 = 24-hour written notice for non-emergency entry.
Email is OK; text is OK; "I'm just stopping by" is NOT a lawful purpose.

**5. Bed bug disclosure.**

Civil Code 1962. Required info sheet to every new tenant before move-in. Most landlords miss this — easy to overlook.

**6. The "I might get sued" file.**

Pull together for every rental:
- Signed leases
- All notices (rent increase, entry, 3-day, etc.)
- All repair requests + completions
- All financial transactions
- All written communications

If a tenant files in small claims (≤$12,500), this file is your entire defense.

**"Does my online prayer actually count?"**

### 正文

Let me try a thoughtful answer.

What counts in Chinese Buddhist practice:

1. **Sincerity of intention (诚意)** — your internal commitment
2. **Correctness of form (如法)** — the ritual structure
3. **Merit generated through the act** — the result of the practice

All three are achievable from anywhere. The form requires a monk at a temple. The sincerity is yours. The merit arises regardless of physical location.

This is why mainstream Chinese Buddhism has allowed remote practice for centuries. Distance is a constraint, not a moral failing.

**The lived question** — what "online prayer" actually is:

- A permanent tablet (牌位) at the temple — your ancestor's name is on the chanting roll
- Sutra recitation by monks at the temple, with your family commission
- Festival observance — Ullambana (盂兰盆), Lunar New Year, winter solstice
- A 49-day cycle of chanting after a death

These are not contingencies. They're standard services in Chinese Buddhist temples — just done for people who are physically distant.

**What's NOT exactly the same:**

- You don't walk up to the altar
- You don't see the temple directly
- You don't interact with the monks in person

Online practice isn't a substitute for all forms. Ordination, monastic training, certain festivals — in-person required.

**But for what most diaspora Chinese need — ancestor veneration, memorial services, family practice — online is the mainstream path.**

I started fopusha.com because my own family needed this. We've worked with verified temples across China, Taiwan, and Malaysia to provide commissionable services — tablets, sutras, festival cycles, annual observances.
